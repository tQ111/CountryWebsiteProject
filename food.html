Western China

Sichuanese/Szechuan Cuisine

This style of Chinese cuisine originates from the Sichuan Province. The food consists of bold flavors. More specifically, a strong and spicy taste due to the use of chili peppers, Sichuan pepper, and garlic.

Northern China

Shandong Cuisine

This cooking style is well known for its variety of cooking methods. This style utilizes domestic animals, birds, seafood, vegetables, and more. Methods of cooking can be frying, boiling, stewing, and roasting. The food is known for its freshness and rich taste.

Eastern China

Huaiyang/Jianghuai Cuisine

This cooking style is derived from the native style of the region surrounding the Huai and Yangtze river. This stle is seen as one of the most popular and prestigious styles of cuisine that dominates the culinary culture of China. The dishes tend to be slightly sweeter than others and contain little to no spice.

Sourthern China & Hong Kong

Yue/Cantonese Cuisine

This style of cuisine represents the south of China. This style is also referred to as Cantonese or Guangdong cuisine. This style of cuisine is one of the most famous and was at a certain time the most popular style throughout the Western world. This cuisine incorporates almost all edible meats and even exotic animals such as snails. This style is also known to be balanced and not greasy.
